Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

The Innovation pillar of "Bangladesh 2.0" envisions a future where technology and creativity intersect to transform the nation. By nurturing groundbreaking ideas and fostering a dynamic ecosystem for research and development, it aims to propel Bangladesh toward global excellence and sustainable prosperity.

Sustainability

The Sustainability pillar of "Bangladesh 2.0" focuses on building a greener, more resilient future by prioritizing environmental stewardship, renewable energy, and resource efficiency. It envisions a balance between economic growth and ecological preservation, ensuring a thriving and sustainable Bangladesh for generations to come.

Infrastructure

The Infrastructure pillar of "Bangladesh 2.0" envisions a modern and interconnected Bangladesh through the development of smart cities, advanced transportation networks, and resilient public utilities. It aims to create a foundation for economic growth, improved quality of life, and seamless integration into the global economy.

Empowerment

The Empowerment pillar of "Bangladesh 2.0" focuses on fostering equal opportunities for all, promoting social inclusion, and empowering individuals through education, skill development, and access to resources. It aims to create a society where every citizen, especially women and marginalized groups, can unlock their full potential and contribute to the nation’s progress.

The Vision of Future

"Bangladesh 2.0" envisions a future driven by innovation, sustainability, and inclusivity, where technological advancements and green development propel the nation towards global leadership. It aims to empower every citizen and build a prosperous, forward-thinking society.

Digital Transformation

"Bangladesh 2.0" aims to leverage digital technologies to enhance governance, improve accessibility, and drive economic growth.

Green Energy

"Bangladesh 2.0" plans to invest in renewable energy solutions to reduce carbon emissions and promote sustainable development.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist, social entrepreneur, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate (2006) renowned for pioneering microfinance and founding Grameen Bank. His work empowers the poor, particularly women, by providing small, collateral-free loans, helping millions escape poverty. He also introduced the concept of social business, addressing societal issues through sustainable enterprises. Globally influential, he authored notable books like "Banker to the Poor" and continues to inspire economic inclusion and sustainable development.
